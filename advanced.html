Advanced Path

1. Security Architect

A Security Architect is responsible for designing secure systems that can resist potential cyber threats. This role involves high-level planning, as well as hands-on work in developing and maintaining secure networks and systems.

  • Key Responsibilities:
    • Designing and implementing security architectures for networks, applications, and cloud environments.
    • Leading security initiatives for system development life cycles (SDLC).
    • Ensuring that security controls align with business objectives and compliance requirements.
  • Skills Required:
    • Expertise in network design, cryptography, and secure coding practices.
    • Knowledge of cloud security (AWS, Azure) and hybrid cloud environments.
    Certifications:

    CISSP, TOGAF, SABSA.

    Average Salary: $120,000 - $160,000 per year.

2. Cybersecurity Manager

Cybersecurity Managers are responsible for leading a team of security professionals, overseeing security operations, and ensuring the organization meets all necessary security standards and regulatory requirements.

  • Key Responsibilities:
    • Managing cybersecurity projects, including deployment of security technologies and practices.
    • Overseeing daily operations, threat monitoring, and incident response efforts.
    • Coordinating with different departments to ensure security policies are followed.
  • Skills Required:
    • Strong leadership, project management, and communication skills.
    • In-depth knowledge of risk management and regulatory compliance (GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS).
    Certifications:

    CISSP, CISM, PMP.

    Average Salary: $110,000 - $150,000 per year.

3. Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

The CISO is the top executive responsible for a company’s overall cybersecurity strategy. They provide leadership in establishing security standards, managing cybersecurity risks, and ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements.

  • Key Responsibilities:
    • Developing and implementing enterprise-wide security strategies.
    • Overseeing all security-related operations and ensuring the protection of sensitive data.
    • Communicating cybersecurity risks and progress to the board of directors and executive leadership.
  • Skills Required:
    • Strategic planning and leadership.
    • Strong understanding of governance, risk management, and compliance (GRC).
    Certifications:

    CISSP, CISM, CRISC.

    Average Salary: $150,000 - $250,000 per year.

4. Threat Hunter

Threat Hunters proactively search for cyber threats that have bypassed traditional security defenses. They investigate anomalous behavior in systems to find evidence of malicious activity.

  • Key Responsibilities:
    • Engaging in proactive threat hunting activities.
    • Using threat intelligence data to identify advanced persistent threats (APTs).
    • Collaborating with incident response teams to contain and neutralize discovered threats.
    Skills Required:
    • Knowledge of malware analysis, reverse engineering, and threat intelligence platforms.
    Certifications:

    GCTI, GCTH.

    Average Salary: $100,000 - $140,000 per year.

5. Cloud Security Engineer

As companies increasingly move to cloud infrastructure, Cloud Security Engineers play a crucial role in ensuring that cloud systems are secure and compliant with relevant laws.

  • Key Responsibilities:
    • Designing and implementing secure cloud infrastructure.
    • Ensuring cloud platforms meet compliance requirements (e.g., GDPR, SOC 2).
    • Managing identity and access management (IAM) in the cloud.
    Skills Required:
    • Proficiency in AWS, Azure, or Google Cloud security tools.
    • Expertise in cloud compliance frameworks and DevSecOps methodologies.
    Certifications:

    CCSP, AWS Certified Security.

    Average Salary: $110,000 - $150,000 per year.

6. Cyber Forensics Analyst

Cyber Forensics Analysts investigate cybercrimes by collecting and analyzing digital evidence. They work closely with law enforcement agencies to help prosecute cybercriminals.

  • Key Responsibilities:
    • Recovering deleted files, decrypting encrypted data, and analyzing digital devices for evidence.
    • Working with law enforcement to build a chain of custody for digital evidence.
    Skills Required:
    • Experience with forensic tools like EnCase or FTK (Forensic Toolkit).
    • Understanding of cyber laws and regulations.
    Certifications:

    GCFE, EnCE.

    Average Salary: $80,000 - $120,000 per year.
